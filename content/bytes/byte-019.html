Byte #019: Good vs. Bad AI Use Cases — How to Tell Them Apart

In the AI era, not all that glitters is gold. The ability to discern effective AI applications from the duds is a critical skill for consultants.

Today’s Byte in a Nutshell: Not every AI use case is a winner. Learn how to differentiate between transformative AI applications and those that overpromise and underdeliver.

The essence of a good AI use case lies in alignment with strategic objectives, data availability, and measurable impact. Bad use cases often stem from misalignment, inadequate data, or unrealistic expectations.

Key indicators of a strong use case include clear problem definition, tangible ROI, and scalability. In contrast, poor use cases often involve ambiguous goals, lack of stakeholder buy-in, or excessive reliance on AI to solve complex human problems without a clear roadmap.

Real-world Consulting Examples:

1. A retailer using AI to optimize inventory management saw a 20% reduction in stockouts, indicating a strategic fit and clear ROI.

2. A financial services firm attempted to use AI for customer service enhancements but faced data privacy issues and unclear objectives, resulting in project suspension.

Why This Matters (to Us):

As consultants, our role is to guide clients towards impactful AI investments, ensuring alignment with their strategic priorities and preparedness for implementation challenges.

Consulting Tip:

Always start with a pilot. Validate assumptions with a small-scale implementation before committing to full-scale deployment.
